Spectral BRDF Simplification

Dataset [awaiting paper acceptance] Code [awaiting paper acceptance]

Abstract

Non-diffuse materials like metallic inks, varnish coatings, and paints are widely used (for example, on e-commerce boxes). Spectral rendering is a significant pre-processing step in the fulfillment process. An important attribute of this is the bidirectional reflection distribution function (BRDF). Current methods of capturing the BRDFs have proven to be onerous in accomplishing quick turnaround time from conception and design, to production. In this paper, we consider the acquisition of 31 wavelengths for four packaging materials. We suggest the use of a multilayer perceptron as a means to provide compact material representations in the much more vast spectral space. Our neural-based scenario suggests the acquisition of far fewer measurements while maintaining significant saliency. We provide adequate results for a range of materials including a complex goniochromatic material.
